<div class="submitted">
<span class="taxonomy">Tags: <ul class="links inline"><li class="taxonomy_term_152 first"><a href="/category/wardraftveteran-affairs" rel="tag" title="">WAR/DRAFT/VETERAN AFFAIRS</a></li>
<li class="taxonomy_term_154 last"><a href="/category/wmd" rel="tag" title="">WMD</a></li>
</ul></span>
</div>
<div class="content">
<p>At some point in the next few weeks, a B-52H bomber will carry a missile high into the air and launch it at an unprecedented velocity toward its target, according to the U.S. Air Force. If everything goes according to plan, that missile will accelerate to more than five times the speed of sound before deploying a dummy second stage that will promptly "disintegrate" somewhere in the atmosphere.</p>
<p>The missile, known as AGM-183A, is supposed to be the first hypersonic weapon — or Air-launched Rapid Response Weapon (ARRW) — in the U.S. arsenal. It should move so quickly through the atmosphere — about 20 times the speed of sound — at such low altitudes that it's impossible for enemy missile defense systems to shoot out of the air. And its speed means that it can be useful for destroying "high-value, time-sensitive targets," the Air Force said in a statement.</p>
<p>Hypersonic missile designs, including this one, typically involve two stages.</p>

<div class="submitted">
<span class="author">By: malterwitty</span><br/>
<span class="taxonomy">Tags: <ul class="links inline"><li class="taxonomy_term_143 first last"><a href="/category/sciencehealthclimatenature" rel="tag" title="">SCIENCE/HEALTH/CLIMATE/NATURE</a></li>
</ul></span>

<div class="content"><p>A giant underwater telescope has been deployed 4,300ft below the surface of Lake Baikal in a bid to observe neutrinos - the smallest particles known to science.</p>
<p>Baikal-GVD has been under construction since 2015 and consists of strings with spherical glass and stainless steel modules measuring a total of 17,657 cubic feet.</p>
<p>A neutrino is a subatomic particle with no electrical charge and a very small mass - they are one of the most abundant particles in the universe but because of their small size they are very difficult to detect.</p>
<p>The new underwater telescope was carefully lowered into a rectangular hole cut in the frozen water two and a half miles off the shore of Lake Baikal.</p>
<p>The team behind the telescope say understanding neutrinos can help reveal details of the early evolution of the universe, dark matter and dark energy.</p>

<h2 class="title"><a href="https://www.dailymail.co.uk/sciencetech/article-9364315/International-Space-Station-releases-2-9-TON-pallet-batteries-orbit-burn-up.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="International Space Station releases 2.9-TON pallet of batteries into orbit that NASA says should burn up ‘harmlessly’ in the atmosphere in two to four years">International Space Station releases 2.9-TON pallet of batteries into orbit that NASA says should burn up ‘harmlessly’ in the atmosphere in two to four years</a></h2>
<div class="submitted">
<span class="author">By: malterwitty</span><br/>
<span class="taxonomy">Tags: <ul class="links inline"><li class="taxonomy_term_70 first last"><a href="/category/current_events" rel="tag" title="">CURRENT EVENTS</a></li>
</ul></span>
</div>
<div class="content"><p>The International Space Station (ISS) has discarded its largest piece of space junk to-date – a 2.9-ton pallet of 48 nickel-hydrogen batteries. </p>
<p>A robotic arm attached to the craft released 265 miles above Earth's surface, which is set to spend the next two to four years in low orbit ‘before burning up harmlessly in the atmosphere.’</p>
<p>NASA, however, does not have data about how many fragments might survive re-entry, NASA communications specialist Leah Cheshier told Spaceflight Now.</p>
<p>Dumping the pallet was not the original plan, as it was set to make a return trip aboard the Japanese H-II Transfer Vehicle (HTV), but it was left behind last year due to a failed 2018 Soyuz launch that disrupted spacewalk schedules.</p>

<h2 class="title"><a href="https://www.dailymail.co.uk/sciencetech/article-9366777/Discovery-new-Dead-Sea-Scroll-fragments-announced-Israel.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Dozens of new Dead Sea Scroll fragments bearing biblical text are discovered in Israel's 'Cave of Horror' having been hidden during a Jewish revolt against Rome 1,900 years ago">Dozens of new Dead Sea Scroll fragments bearing biblical text are discovered in Israel's 'Cave of Horror' having been hidden during a Jewish revolt against Rome 1,900 years ago</a></h2>
<div class="submitted">
<span class="author">By: malterwitty</span><br/>
<span class="taxonomy">Tags: <ul class="links inline"><li class="taxonomy_term_70 first last"><a href="/category/current_events" rel="tag" title="">CURRENT EVENTS</a></li>
</ul></span>
</div>
<div class="content"><p>The fragments include lines of Greek text from the biblical books of Zechariah and Nahum and were radiocarbon dated to the 2nd century AD, experts confirmed.  </p>
<p>The Israel Antiquities Authority say more than 20 fragments were found in a remote canyon in the Judean Desert, south of Jerusalem - the first find of its kind in 60 years.</p>
<p>They were discovered in the Cave of Horror, named in the 1950s after 40 skeletons of women, men and children were found after excavations - they were hiding from Roman soldiers during the 2nd century Jewish Bar Kochba Revolt.</p>
<p>To get into the cave, which is well secluded from view and difficult to access, teams of archaeologists and other experts had to repel down the side of a 260ft cliff. </p>
<p>A number of items were found in the cave, including a 10,000 year old woven basket, the skeleton of a girl dating back 6,000 years and the biblical fragments.</p>
